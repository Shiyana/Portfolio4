Process

Data Collection and Assessment

Sourced a retail dataset with income, spending score, and age variables. Identified 5% missing data and outliers (z-score > 3) using pandas and visualization tools like Seaborn heatmaps.

Handling Missing Data

Applied median imputation for income and multiple imputation (via MICE) for cross-validation, inspired by Scenario 1’s health study approach. Added missingness flags to track potential bias, aligning with ethical auditing practices. [Graph 1: Histogram of income before and after imputation, showing how the distribution shifts from a skewed pattern with gaps (due to 5% missing data) to a smoother curve after median imputation at approximately $50,000, demonstrating the effectiveness of the imputation strategy in preserving data integrity.]

Data Cleaning

Used a 3-point rolling average and z-score filtering to smooth spending score fluctuations and remove outliers (e.g., incomes > $500,000), building on Scenario 2’s noise reduction strategies. Validated results with pre- and post-cleaning plots. [Graph 2: Line plot of spending scores over time, comparing raw data with smoothed data, showing a reduction in random spikes (e.g., from 90 to 60) and a stabilized trend around 50 after smoothing, confirming the robustness of the cleaning process.]

Clustering Implementation

Determined k=4 clusters using the elbow method and silhouette scores, applying K-Means iteratively. Adjusted based on segment interpretability, reflecting real-world validation needs.

Exploration of Imbalance

Tested SMOTE to synthesize data points and class weights in a mock supervised model, drawing from Scenario 3’s rare disease focus, to explore fairness in segmentation.
