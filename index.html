Overview

This extended overview presents a deep dive into unsupervised learning using the K-Means clustering algorithm. The foundation of this project was built upon practical exercises completed through a guided machine learning chatbot activity, where I navigated three critical scenarios: handling missing data, noisy data, and class imbalance. These scenarios provided insights into the complex world of real-world data preprocessing and model readiness.

Scenario 1: Missing Data — We often encounter incomplete datasets in real-world applications. I explored strategies like deletion (listwise and pairwise), mean/mode/median imputation, and more advanced methods such as k-nearest neighbor imputation and regression-based imputation. This helped me understand which technique is appropriate based on the nature of the missingness (MCAR, MAR, MNAR).

Scenario 2: Noisy Data — Noise in data often masks trends. I used visualization (scatter plots, boxplots), outlier detection (z-score, IQR), and smoothing methods (moving average, Savitzky-Golay) to denoise data. Noise reduction is vital before clustering to avoid misinterpretation and incorrect segment identification.

Scenario 3: Class Imbalance — While not directly applicable to K-Means, the understanding of SMOTE, undersampling, and ensemble balancing gave me clarity on feature representation across clusters and avoiding overrepresented clusters dominating the results.

Furthermore, the integration of ethical leadership, human bias, and fairness in AI reinforced how technical decisions influence real lives. In sectors like education and healthcare, misclustered outputs can misinform resource allocation or service eligibility. This artifact not only tested my technical ability but also my ethical discernment. I designed clustering workflows that minimized bias propagation and preserved group fairness.

By the end of this section, I had built confidence in preparing raw data for analysis and understood the broader implications of model assumptions and outcomes. This holistic understanding positions me to contribute meaningfully to AI/ML teams that value both precision and people.
