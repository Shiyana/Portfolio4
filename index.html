Overview

This artifact demonstrates how K-Means clustering was applied to segment customer data and discover hidden patterns. It highlights the role of unsupervised learning in business analytics and customer behavior segmentation.

Process

Step 1: Cleaned and normalized the dataset.
Step 2: Used Elbow and Silhouette methods to determine optimal clusters (k=3).
Step 3: Applied K-Means algorithm and labeled clusters.
Step 4: Visualized clusters for interpretation.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Cluster Selection: Resolved by comparing Elbow and Silhouette scores.
  • Outliers: Preprocessed data to remove anomalies for better clustering.

Key Takeaways

  • Developed hands-on skills with unsupervised learning.
  • Enhanced visualization techniques for clustering insights.
  • Learned to balance preprocessing with interpretability.

Reflection

This artifact helped reinforce my understanding of unsupervised learning, particularly in business applications. The clustering model I built offered actionable insights about customer segmentation. One of the most valuable takeaways was learning how the preprocessing decisions—like scaling or handling outliers—can dramatically impact cluster quality. I also gained experience tuning the number of clusters and interpreting visual outputs to inform business decisions.

Conclusion & Future Work

This project deepened my understanding of clustering algorithms and their value in real-world data scenarios. In the future, I plan to explore hierarchical clustering and integrate results into an interactive business dashboard.
